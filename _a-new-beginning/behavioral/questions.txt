Tell me about a time when you failed or made a wrong call. What did you learn?

Early in one of my projects, I designed a deeply nested component and folder structure inspired by how the UI was visually organized. My intention was to maintain strict separation and scalability. However, after implementing the first full page, it became clear that this structure made component discovery difficult, increased nesting complexity, and slowed down development due to long import paths.
Rather than continuing down that path, I proactively raised the concern, gathered feedback from the team, and refactored into a flatter, developer-centric structure where all related composable components lived in a single directory per page. This reduced friction, made onboarding easier, and sped up development significantly.
That experience taught me a critical lesson: scalable architecture is not just about theoretical best practices‚Äîit must also optimize developer productivity and real-world maintainability. Since then, I always validate architectural decisions with a ‚Äòdeveloper-first‚Äô lens and ensure ease of adoption is part of the design criteria..

Another instance was to decide on how to build chat like form creation using decision tree. I decided to build it using self implementation that gave me great control but it introduced too much complexity to handle different kind of similar forms with custom components as message bubble. Though it was working fine but i felt using any existing library like react chatbotify would have been better suited.

That decision saved weeks of future development time and enabled other teams to contribute easily without needing to learn D3.
